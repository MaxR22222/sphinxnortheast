    Anita and Arabella

    Upstate Art Weekend
    Catskill Octogon House
    21 Walnut St., Catskill, NY
    7/22-7/23, 11-6 pm


    Sigga Björg Sigurðardóttir and Mikael Lind
    Jesse Bransford

    Two orbweaver spiders were launched into orbit in the summer of 1973, and their webs were observed. They were named Arabella and Anita, as all things in space must be named. The two spiders floated in a maze of peculiar lighting -- as anticipated, their weaving was strange, as though hunger could ever be etched in violet light.

    Without the sensation of earth's gravity, each web was a whirligig, suspended not in dawn or day. Arabella and Anita’s stage is recurrent and sacerdotal, and at the Octagon House we return to assumed and ambiguous forms: the spiral, the wheel, the line, the web. Fifty years later, we salute these two arachnids -- spinning their names, no matter gravity or microgravity. We go in circles, break silver threads, and trace the webs' imperfect topos.
